# 20 Fun Facts About The Hill Family
*Pulled from the Hill Family Jeopardy! game*

1. **Aaron Hill**, born in 1838, was known throughout the community as "**The Community Counselor**" — whenever there was a dispute, neighbors would send for Aaron.

2. Aaron and Mary Ann Hill raised **14 children** — seven sons (Charlie, Arnold, Willie, Wesley, Bubba, Boise, and Myers) and seven daughters (Sarah, Anni, Ardina, Isabell, Mary, Lillie, and Rosa).

3. Aaron and Mary Ann were married in **1863** — the very same year slavery ended in America. The Hill family legacy was built entirely in freedom.

4. In 1876, Aaron Hill was one of only **three people** Harriet Myers, First Lady of the Myers property, trusted to witness her Will.

5. In 1886, **Charlie Hill rowed a group of millionaires to Jekyll Island by rowboat** so they could survey the land — shortly after, they purchased the island for $125,000.

6. Charlie Hill's famous call, "**The Wheels are rolling at 4!**" became legend — if the Maurice family wasn't ready when he brought the stagecoach, he'd leave without them.

7. Jekyll Island's employee housing was called "**Red Row**" — named for the red tin roofs on the white, green-trimmed homes the millionaires built for their Black employees.

8. An incredible **77% of all Jekyll Island employees** were connected to Charlie Hill in some way — he got jobs for his family, in-laws, and friends.

9. **Earl Hill helped desegregate golf in Georgia in 1964**, using his connections with sports figures — including NFL legend Jim Brown — and Atlanta preachers.

10. **Barbara Hill Floyd founded the very first Hill Family Reunion** in Brunswick, Georgia, held August 7–9, 1992.

11. **W. Ray Hill** served with the 97th Engineers Regiment in Valdez, Alaska, helping survey and construct the **Alcan Highway** during WWII.

12. After the war, **W. Ray Hill became the personal manager for comedian Redd Foxx** — one of America's most beloved entertainers.

13. At the Jekyll Island schoolhouse in the 1930s, **Anna Hill's students had to memorize every U.S. state, capital, mountain, valley, and river** — up through sixth grade.

14. **Barbara Hill Floyd was the very first person hired as a maid** at the Wanderer Hotel (now Comfort Inn) on Jekyll Island in 1959, and later ran the Women's Golf Auxiliary Tournaments for 25 consecutive years.

15. The Hill family's ancestors are connected to the **Gullah/Geechee Nation**, recognized by the U.S. government as a national ethnic group, known for sweetgrass basketry and a unique Creole language.

16. **The Wanderer**, a ship that landed at Jekyll Island in 1858, illegally carried approximately **409 enslaved people** from the Kingdom of Kongo — 50 years after the Atlantic slave trade to the U.S. was banned.

17. The Jekyll Island Club was purchased for **$125,000 in 1886** — and sold to the state of Georgia for **$675,000 in 1947** (about $10 million today).

18. **Sam Denegal**, "Foreman of the Forest" on Jekyll Island, was so trusted by the millionaires that they named a road after him and tipped him $500 a year — worth about $7,840 today.

19. **Kenneth Parland** made history as the first African American Police Lieutenant in West Long Branch, New Jersey — and still owns the original tools his ancestor Page Parland used on Jekyll Island in the early 1900s.

20. Barbara Hill Floyd wrote an acrostic poem spelling **H-I-L-L**: Humble, Ideals for our Faith, Loyalty to our family, and **Love** to one another near and far.
